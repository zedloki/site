Nu Gamma Chapter

Long ago, when the engineer's struggle was theirs, and theirs alone, came brave souls who saw the power in unity. Within the halls of the Watson engineering building, these upstarts recruited and bred the finest engineers to walk their halls. Though small at first, their influence grew and garnered the attention of the men and women who called themselves Theta Tau. It was then, that Nu Gamma chapter had become the latest example of the legacy left behind by Theta Tau's founders. On the day it was established, so were the names of legacy that head the lineages that each brother holds with pride: Fazio, Tuholski, Hungerford, Balicki, Creager, Abhyankar, Abdur-Rahim, Morrissey, and McDermott.


In 2009, Nu Gamma chapter no longer accepted young women as candidates due to the arrival of a Sorority for female Engineers. Theta Tau's female alumni are still treasured brothers of this fraternity, but the chapter's active members are only male.

Our Nu Gamma chapter at Binghamton University was formally established on May 3, 2003. Since then, our fraternity has placed the highest importance on building brothers and those who aspire to be successful into all that they can be. Erich Julius Schrader's values are as strong with this chapter as any other, as we have a number of brothers dedicated to each indivdual aspect of Theta Tau. We intend to continue this proud history on this day, and every day after in order to produce the most successful minds of engineering and the new age.
